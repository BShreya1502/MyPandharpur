Lord Vitthal : The heart of Pandharpur


Lord Vitthal Pandurang is a Hindu god who is believed to be the incarnation of Lord Vishnu. He is worshiped as the supreme god in Maharashtra and parts of Karnataka. He is also known as Vithoba, Pandharinath, Shri Vitthala and Panduranga.

The history of Lord Vitthal Pandurang can be traced back to the 13th century when he was first mentioned in a Marathi saint's poem called 'Dnyaneshwari'.

Since then, he has been worshipped by millions of Hindus all over India. There are many temples dedicated to him across Maharashtra and Karnataka, but the most famous one is located at Pandharpur in Solapur district.

Meaning of the Word Vitthala: The word Vitthala is made of two words, Vit and thala or sthala. It is a Marathi word, “Vit” means a brick, and “sthala” means a place or standing. Therefore, the word Vitthala means “the one who is standing on a brick”.

Different name of Panduranga : Vithhala, Vithoba, Pandharinath (The lord of Pandharpur), Hari.

Goddess Rakmini : Mother of Universe


Rukmini (Sanskrit: रुक्मिणी, IAST: Rukmini, lit. 'radiant') is a Hindu goddess and the first queen and chief wife of Krishna.In Vaishnava tradition, she is described as Krishna's principal queen in Dvaraka, as well as the chief of his wives. She is an incarnation of the goddess of prosperity, Lakshmi.

The goddess is regarded to be the chief or principal consort of Krishna in various pieces of literature and is venerated primarily in Warkari and Haridasa tradition, and additionally in Sri Vaishnavism where Lakshmi-Narayana are revered and worshipped.

The name Rukmini is derived from the Sanskrit word Rukma which means 'Radiant', 'Clear' or 'Bright'. The name can also mean 'decorated with gold ornaments'. Other names and epithets include:

Shree – Lakshmi, Goddess of Fortune Ruciranana – One Who Has A Beautiful Face, Expanding Like A Lotus Flower Vaidarbhi – She Who Is From The Kingdom Of Vidarbha. Bhaishmi – Daughter of Bhishmaka. Rakhumai – Mother Rukmini. Chiryauvana – One Who Is Forever Young.

Pandharpur Wari - Biggest festival of Devotees


Pandharpur Wari or Wari is a yatra to Pandharpur, Maharashtra, to honor Vithoba. It involves carrying the paduka of a saint in a palkhi, most notably of Dnyaneshwar and Tukaram, from their respective shrines to Pandharpur. Many pilgrims join this procession on foot. Warkari is a Marathi term which means "one who performs the wari". The tradition is more than 700 to 800 years old. Marches happen on foot from various locations in Maharashtra to Vithoba Temple. The journey takes 21 days.

Many palkis join the main Tukaram and Dnyaneshwar palkhis along the way. Two specific pilgrimages honor the two most revered palkhis, starting from their towns located in Pune district of Maharashtra: Sant Dnyaneshwar's palkhi leaves from Alandi, while Tukaram's begins at Dehu. The wari culminates at the Vithoba Temple on the holy occasion of Shayani Ekadashi. Devotees from across Maharashtra and nearby areas leave for Pandharpur, wearing holy basil beads and singing the glories of Vithoba and songs like "Gyanba Tukaram", commemorating the saints. When they reach Pandharpur on Shayani Ekadashi, the devotees bathe in the sacred Bhima River before visiting the temple.

Vitthal - Rukmini temple

The Vithoba Temple, officially known as Shri Vitthal-Rukmini Mandir is a Hindu temple in Pandharpur, in the Indian state of Maharashtra. It is the main centre of worship for Vithoba, a form of the god Vishnu or Krishna, and his consort Rakhumai.

The temple was built by King Vishnuvardhana of Hoysala Empire between 1108–1152 CE upon being convinced by the historical figure Pundalik. Although parts of the temple date to the 12th or 13th century, the existing structure mainly dates to the 17th century or later, and reflects the later Deccan style, with dome motifs and lobed arches.

Kaikadi Maharaj Math

This is a unique place in Solapur town, where almost all important Hindu scriptures and tales therefrom are depicted in the form of scale models, some looking even real size or even bigger.

The display was arranged in such a way that one who joins the queue would have no option to go back but move only forward till he completes the whole show, winding through narrow corridors or tunnels or passages. It can easily take about three hours before one gets the hang of the place.

Chandrabhaga River

The Bhima River (also known as Chandrabhaga River) is a major river in Western India and South India. It flows southeast for 861 kilometres (535 mi) through Maharashtra, Karnataka, and Telangana states, before entering the Krishna River. After the first sixty-five kilometers in a narrow valley through rugged terrain,[1] the banks open up and form a fertile agricultural area which is densely populated.

The river is also referred to as Chandrabhaga River, especially at Pandharpur, as it resembles the shape of the Moon.

Sant Janabai Mandir, Gopalpur

An image found in Hindu temples shows a god and woman grinding together at a mill. This is the revered Hindu sant (holy person) called Janabai. Janabai was a low-caste maidservant and poet. There are various versions of her story, but according to one of these she was taken as a five-year-old child to the temple of the god Vitthal or Vithoba in the city of Pandharpur, the centre of religious devotion for the Varkari Hindu tradition.

She refused to leave, telling her parents that although they loved her, they would in time have to give her away in marriage, and instead she wished to remain in the temple and devote herself to God.

Vishnupad, Gopalpur

The Vishnupad Temple, which is nearly a kilometer south of the Pundalik Temple, is one of the most renowned pilgrim and tourist place and is visited by several pilgrims every year. This temple resides on the bank of the River Chandrabhaga and is connected by a causeway to the shore.

The other attractive thing in the temple is different statues of Lord Vishnu made of stone depicted in various poses. The temple homes three rocks with footprints of Lord Krishna and of a cow embedded in them. There are two types of footprints of Lord Krishna; one stone carries the print of both feet on standing position and the other carries the standing position of left foot with right foot crossed and resting on the toes..
